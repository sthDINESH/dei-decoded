What is DEI?

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion—the commitment to creating a workplace where everyone is respected, valued, and supported, regardless of background or identity.

Diversity

The presence of different backgrounds, experiences, perspectives, and characteristics within a group or organization. This includes but is not limited to race, ethnicity, gender, age, sexual orientation, disability, religion, and socioeconomic status.

Inclusion

The practice of ensuring that people with different identities feel welcomed, valued, and supported. It means creating an environment where everyone can contribute their best work and be their authentic selves.

Equity

Providing fair treatment, access, and opportunities while acknowledging that people start from different places and may need different resources to achieve similar outcomes.

Belonging

The feeling of being an accepted and valued member of a group. When people feel a sense of belonging, they are more likely to contribute their unique perspectives and perform at their best.

Cultural Competence

The ability to interact effectively with people from different cultural backgrounds. It involves awareness, knowledge, and skills to communicate and work across cultural differences.

Accessibility

Designing environments, products, and services that can be used by people with a wide range of abilities and disabilities. This ensures equal access and participation for everyone.

5 Steps to Build Inclusive Spaces

Creating truly inclusive spaces takes intention, empathy, and action.

Five practical steps to take to foster belonging, celebrate diversity, and ensure every voice is valued.

1

Assess Your Current Environment

  • Conduct anonymous surveys to understand employee experiences
  • Review existing policies and practices for potential bias
  • Identify gaps and opportunities for improvement
  • Analyze demographic data and representation metrics
2

Set Clear Goals and Metrics

  • Define specific, measurable diversity and inclusion objectives
  • Establish baseline measurements for tracking progress
  • Create accountability structures with regular check-ins
  • Align D&I goals with overall business strategy
3

Provide Education and Training

  • Implement bias awareness and mitigation programs
  • Offer cultural competency workshops and training
  • Create safe spaces for dialogue and learning
  • Provide leadership training on inclusive management
4

Update Policies and Practices

  • Review and revise hiring and promotion processes
  • Ensure inclusive communication in all materials
  • Implement flexible work arrangements and accommodations
  • Update performance review criteria to include inclusive behaviors
5

Monitor Progress and Iterate

  • Conduct regular surveys and feedback sessions
  • Adjust strategies based on data and employee input
  • Celebrate successes and learn from challenges
  • Continuously refine and improve your approach
