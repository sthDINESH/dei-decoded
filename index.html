What is DEI?

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion—the commitment to creating a workplace where everyone is respected, valued, and supported, regardless of background or identity.

Diversity

The presence of different backgrounds, experiences, perspectives, and characteristics within a group or organization. This includes but is not limited to race, ethnicity, gender, age, sexual orientation, disability, religion, and socioeconomic status.

Inclusion

The practice of ensuring that people with different identities feel welcomed, valued, and supported. It means creating an environment where everyone can contribute their best work and be their authentic selves.

Equity

Providing fair treatment, access, and opportunities while acknowledging that people start from different places and may need different resources to achieve similar outcomes.

Belonging

The feeling of being an accepted and valued member of a group. When people feel a sense of belonging, they are more likely to contribute their unique perspectives and perform at their best.

Cultural Competence

The ability to interact effectively with people from different cultural backgrounds. It involves awareness, knowledge, and skills to communicate and work across cultural differences.

Accessibility

Designing environments, products, and services that can be used by people with a wide range of abilities and disabilities. This ensures equal access and participation for everyone.
